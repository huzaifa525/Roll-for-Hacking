Grand Theft Auto: SAN ANDREAS

Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas is a 2004 action-adventure game developed by Rockstar North and published by Rockstar Games. It is the seventh title in the Grand Theft Auto series, and the follow-up to the 2002 game Grand Theft Auto: Vice City.

Five years ago, Carl Johnson fled the stresses of life in Los Santos, San Andreas, a city that was torn apart by gang violence, narcotics, and corruption. ... CJ is forced on a journey that will take him across the state of San Andreas to save his family and take control of the streets.

Vehicles

The speed, durability and rarity (the vehicle's chances of turning up) differ quite a bit in San Andreas. Many vehicles only exist in areas of San Andreas (such as the Ranger, which is limited to the state's bequests), while others are widespread across San Andreas. The SAM program makes it easy to find out what vehicles tend to be pie. Only click Locate on the SAM! To be led to the San Andreas Map page, with the proper filters applied-this lets you find something in a shot.



Gang Wars

Gang Warfare is a feature of Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas available after completion of Doberman (they are no longer available after the Green Saber mission, but they return after the Home Coming mission). You gain more respect and money by taking over enemy gang territories.

Minigames

Numerous mini-games are available to play in San Andreas, including basketball, pool, rhythm-based challenges (dance and "bouncing" low-ride hydraulics), poker and video game machines that pay tribute to classic arcade games. There are also the casino games and gambling methods mentioned above, such as betting on virtual horse races.

Car Modifications

Some of the cars in the game can be changed and updated to different garages. All car upgrades are purely visual apart from the stereo system and the nitrous oxide upgrade, which improves the bass and enhances the car's speed when activated; and hydraulics, which reduces the car's height by default and enables the player to control different aspects of the car's suspension. Other common modifications include painting jobs, rims, body kits, side skirts, bumpers and spoilers.
